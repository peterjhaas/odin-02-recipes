Black Caviar Lentil

Description

As someone who grew up on dusty, brown food co-op lentils, tasting new-crop lentils was a rvelation. They have an interesting texture, excellent flavor, and they cook lightening fast, at least compared to other beans. These Black Caviar Lentils have a little something extra that makes me sigh with a tinge of regret when I eat them: Why don't I make them more often? In my case, it's because I have 35 other varieties of legumes to rotate through, but these are one of my favorites.

We don't mention as often as we should, but once cooked, they're cute! I love a little mound of them on a composed salad. Theyre also great in a salt of grated carrots. The orange carrots and black lentils make for a good platter!

Black Caviar Lentils with Roasted Tomatoes and Garlic

This seems like a great side dish to serve with fish or chicken, but could also be delicous tossed with fresh pasta. Roast some extra cherry tomatoes if you can --- they are flavor-packed jewels that can be used in salads, pasta, and of course, with your favorite legumes.

Ingredients

  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil, plus more for drizzling
  • 2 garlic gloves, minced, plus 1 whole garlic clove
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground pepper
  • 1 cup uncooked Rancho Gordo Black Caviar Lentils, picked over and rinsed
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 6 to 8 ounches fresh mozzarella balls (bocconcini)
  • 2 tablespoon good-quality balsamic vinegar, plus more if needed.
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh basil

Makes 4 side servings

Steps

  • Prehead the oven to 350Â°F. In a baking dish large enough to hold the tomoates in a single layer, mix the tomatoes with about 2 tablespoons olive oil, the minced garlic, and a generous pinch of salt. Arrange the tomatoes cut side up and roast until they start to shrivel and caramelize, 30 to 45 minutes.
  • Meanwhile, cook the lentils: Place in a pot and add enough water to cover by about 2 inches. Add the bay leaf and the whole garlic clove. Bring to a full boil, then reduce heat to a gentle simmer, using a lid to help regulate the heat, and gently cook until done, 25 to 30 minutes. Drain excess liquid, then salt and pepper to taste.
  • Remove the baking dish from the oven and add the cooked lentils, cheese, and vinegar; gently combine. Drizzle with more olive oil. Bake for another 15 minutes.
  • Remove from the oven and sprinkle with salt, pepper, and more balsamic vinegar, if desired. Add the basil and genly toss. Serve warm.
